Help army

Weaving of camouflage nets

January 10, 2022

During the full-scale war, I was chosen as the leader of our student council's volunteer group at my school. This started a journey that deeply impacted my sense of community and togetherness. At the beginning, my main focus was making camouflage nets in a nearby church. It felt like a beautiful harmony of threads, echoing our shared cause. At the same time, I got involved in making trench candles, which were vital for the soldiers. These small lights symbolized hope and resilience. One of my proudest moments was inspiring my fellow students to volunteer. Together, we turned our passion into action. Every month, we created several camouflage nets and hundreds of trench candles. This showed the power of working together for a common goal, even in challenging times. Our contributions, like the comforting glow of a trench candle, offered hope in a world filled with uncertainty and conflict.

About my dance

I fell in love with folk dancing at the tender age of 6, and that passion has been a constant in my life ever since. This art form, with its dynamic movements and lively rhythms, allowed me to experience the joys of freedom and the warmth of a close-knit community.
Throughout my dancing journey, I had the opportunity to perform on the grand stages of theaters, feeling the energy of the audience as we brought our routines to life. I also tested my skills in thrilling dance competitions, where the adrenaline of the challenge pushed me to excel.
My love for folk dancing extended beyond the borders of my country. I embarked on international tours and took part in competitions abroad, which broadened my horizons and allowed me to connect with people from diverse cultures who shared a common language—the language of dance.
Today, folk dancing remains an integral part of my identity. It has shaped my character, instilled in me the values of discipline and creativity, and continues to bring joy, connection, and a sense of belonging to my life.

The expedition through the realms of the English language

I've cherished the English language since childhood, embarking on my learning journey at the age of 5.
By the time I turned 14, I had already attained an Upper-Intermediate level, yet faced challenges in speaking.
A pivotal international internship greatly refined my spoken English, and I forged lasting friendships with individuals from the USA, Canada, and Australia.
Together, we continued to immerse ourselves in the language during our free time.
My initial teaching experience unfolded amidst the Russian invasion of Ukraine, where I taught English to children from the occupied territories.
Subsequently, I received a job offer at the Dnipro English Language School, allowing me to refine my skills working with diverse age groups and training levels.
Currently, I am guiding 7 regular students in preparation for various school and international exams, augmenting their general English proficiency through my unique methods and interactive games.

Humanitarian aid for homeless pets

October 2, 2020

Our group of caring volunteers stepped up to help homeless dogs and cats in our community. We began organizing weekly meetings where students from our pre-university program joined me in supporting the shelter workers who took care of these animals. During these gatherings, we focused on showing empathy and responsibility. We did things like feeding the hungry animals and taking them for walks, giving them the simple gift of companionship. These weekly meetings weren't just a routine; they were proof of how powerful teamwork and kindness can be. Together, we offered these animals a glimmer of hope, making sure they felt remembered in a world that often ignores their struggles. Our efforts brought joy and purpose to our lives, highlighting the significant impact that a small, dedicated group can have in improving the lives of those in need.

Humanitarian aid for eco-park

October 3, 2020

At the forefront of this humanitarian mission, I took on the responsibility of coordinating the collection and delivery of aid for the cherished eco-park. This sanctuary, once celebrated as a haven for biodiversity and a source of national pride, had been tragically scarred by the harsh realities of war. In the face of adversity, its animal residents, who had once thrived in their natural habitat, were forced to abandon their familiar surroundings and seek refuge in a neighboring city. Our determination to provide assistance to this iconic eco-park extended beyond mere logistics. We believed in the restorative power of collective action and the resilience of both nature and the human spirit. As we gathered vital supplies and mobilized our resources, we were fueled by the hope of breathing new life into this now-silent sanctuary. Our mission embodied the spirit of unity and compassion, serving as a testament to the unwavering commitment to both the environment and the well-being of the creatures who call it home.

Open door day in my pre-university

October 4, 2020

I've actively taken part in various events at my school, and one of them stands out – Open Door Day. It was a day filled with excitement and a clear purpose. During this event, I had the role of a friendly host, aiming to inspire potential students to explore what our school had to offer. As students and parents visited our campus, I welcomed them with a warm smile and a genuine desire to share my experiences. It was a chance to connect on a personal level, to bridge the gap between curiosity and information. I patiently answered their questions and provided guidance to help these students and their families make informed choices about their education. Participating in Open Door Day was more than just a role for me; it was an opportunity to inspire and guide others, just as I had been supported in my educational journey. This experience strengthened my commitment to making a positive impact on those around me and giving back to the educational community.

Refurbishment of my pre-university

October 4, 2020

Apart from helping others, I also joined in renovating my school. It wasn't just about fixing up the place; it was about making our learning environment better. We worked as a team, painting walls, fixing things, and sprucing up areas that needed attention. This effort brought us together and made us feel like we were improving our own space. This renovation was more than just a physical change; it was about creating a comfortable, inspiring place for students. As we saw the school transform, we also saw a positive change in our school spirit. It reminded us that even small efforts could have a big impact on our school and the people in it.

Victories in volunteer activities

October 4, 2020

Behind the scenes, I took some actions to help during a challenging time. I asked the Headmaster if we could open our school's canteen to serve food to refugees, military personnel, and the elderly. I got chefs and students on board to make it happen, and it turned out to be a big help during the tough times of war. I also played a role in packing and giving out humanitarian aid to refugees. I chose clothes for each person and had conversations with folks from occupied areas to provide some comfort and distraction from the difficult war situations.
